# 3️⃣ Core Governance Principles

In `governance_principles.md`, define:

1. **Transparency** – All scoring methodologies public.
2. **Reproducibility** – Certification results reproducible.
3. **Independence** – Certification separated from revenue influence.
4. **Proportionality** – Risk-based oversight.
5. **Data Minimization** – No unnecessary data retention.
6. **Adaptability** – Policy evolution but bounded.
7. **Global Neutrality** – No jurisdictional favoritism.
8. **Auditability** – All governance decisions logged.

---

# 4️⃣ Separation of Powers Model

Define 4 bodies:

---

## 4.1 Certification Authority

* Issues certifications
* Executes evaluation engine
* Publishes registry

Cannot modify thresholds without approval.

---

## 4.2 Policy Council

* Maintains GSS standard
* Proposes threshold changes
* Oversees risk classification evolution

Requires advisory review.

---

## 4.3 Advisory Board

* External experts
* Reviews governance changes
* Can veto threshold adjustments
* Oversees conflict-of-interest cases

---

## 4.4 Alliance Council

* Represents ecosystem members
* Proposes working group updates
* Reviews marketplace policy

---

# 5️⃣ Checks & Balances

Document in:

```
checks_and_balances.md
```

Examples:

* Certification body cannot change policy.
* Policy council cannot issue certifications.
* Advisory board must approve major threshold shifts.
* Marketplace cannot influence certification logic.
* Public transparency report mandatory quarterly.

Create:

```
amendment_process.md
```

2. Impact simulation run.
3. Advisory review.
4. Public notice period.
